Scarlett O'Hara's Mansion in Charleston, SC

Scarlett O'Hara's and Rhett'ss Mansion, known as the "Tara" plantation in the classic novel "Gone with the Wind" by Margaret Mitchell, is a historic landmark located in Charleston, South Carolina.

The mansion, with its iconic white columns and sprawling gardens, is a symbol of Southern charm and elegance. Visitors can explore the beautifully landscaped grounds and learn about the history of the mansion, which served as the backdrop for many dramatic scenes in the novel and its film adaptation.

Today, Scarlett O'Hara's Mansion is open to the public, allowing fans of the book and movie to step back in time and experience the grandeur of the Old South. Guided tours provide insight into the history of the mansion and its significance in American literature and cinema.

If you're planning a trip to Charleston, don't miss the opportunity to visit this iconic Southern mansion and immerse yourself in the world of Scarlett O'Hara and "Gone with the Wind."
